# Design Research — Reference Sites (CEO picks)

Studied live: clay.com, miro.com, notion.com, similarweb.com (July 2026).

## Per-site notes

**Clay** — 3D illustrated hero world over full-bleed color; huge tight grotesk headline; split hero (headline left / one-liner + dual CTA right); cream base, white rounded cards; proof wall immediately after hero with logos *and* metric callouts ("+140% outbound pipeline"); chip-tab selector for use cases; product UI in rounded frames.

**Miro** — homepage *is* the product: live canvas hero with named collaborative cursors drawing in real time. Yellow accent only in tiny moments; logo wall (Sony, PayPal, Deloitte); alternating text/product feature sections behind tab selector.

**Notion** — pure white; massive black grotesk headline with an inline visual token (green "● Ship" pill inside the sentence); character illustrations for warmth; dual CTA (solid + tinted); immediate product screenshot; logo wall at the fold.

**Similarweb** — dark hero, gradient emphasis on key headline words; interactive widget *inside the hero* (try the product in 5s); fanned layered screenshots; strong primary vs outline secondary CTA.

## Shared patterns

1. Neutral base + one scarce accent color.
2. The real product visible within the first screen.
3. Immediate social proof (logo wall / metric cards) under the hero.
4. Huge, tight, sentence-case grotesk headlines.
5. Dual CTA ("try" + "talk"), chip/tab selectors to compress use cases.
6. One playful signature element for humanity (3D world / characters / cursors).

## Applied to nphones.com

- Hero headline scaled up + tighter tracking (Notion/Clay proportions)
- Notion-style inline token: teal "● one device" pill inside the headline
- Proof strip under hero (team pedigree wordmarks until customer logos exist)
- Homepage use-case chip switcher (Clay/Miro pattern)
- Hero phone demonstrates the personal↔business switch live in the hero (Miro's product-as-hero lesson)
